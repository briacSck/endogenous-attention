# E1 + E2 — annotated (run of 2026-07-07, n = 10 kappa values)

Raw: `e1_e2_raw.md` / `e1_rows.csv`. Pre-registration:

## H1 — CONFIRMED: as-if failure tracks attention repricing
**Spearman(|m1* − m0*|, as-if CF RMSE) = 0.88.** The corner structure is
exactly as pre-registered: for kappa ≤ 0.05 attention is pinned at m = 1 pre
and post (|dm| = 0) and the as-if counterfactual is fine (RMSE ≈ 0.0045,
the clone baseline); in the interior, failure rises with the repricing
(kappa = 0.1: |dm| = 0.15, RMSE 0.0161 — 3.6× the corner). theta2_hat
tracks m0 across the grid (1.53 at m = 1 down to 0.19 at m = 0.2): the
as-if absorption of paper #1, reproduced — and now shown to be exactly what
breaks when the primitive moves.

## H2 — CONFIRMED: the pre-policy audit is blind
**Spearman(conformity score, as-if RMSE) = −0.09.** The per-channel
dispersion audits are flat across the kappa grid (cv_x ≈ 0.69–0.79, cv_c ≈
0.53–0.64) — every fixed-m sparse-max agent's internals genuinely conform
to the model class pre-policy, so the audit correctly reports "conforming"
and carries no information about the coming failure. The failure lives one
level up (the primitive's response to the intervention), which no
pre-policy internal measurement can see. This is the boundary result the
paper is about.

## Honest magnitudes
The attention-repricing failures are real but moderate (max RMSE 0.016,
replacement-rate errors ~0.001) — an order of magnitude smaller than paper
#1's out-of-class categorical failures (up to 0.25). The note should say
so plainly: with THIS calibration, moving the primitive hurts less than
misspecifying the mechanism class. The contribution is the pattern (0.88
vs −0.09), the hierarchy logic, and E3's constructive fix, not the raw
size of the bias.
