# E3 — annotated (runs of 2026-07-07): H3 REJECTED for two-step deep
# estimation, and why that is the finding

Raw: `e3_raw.md` / `e3_rows.csv`. Pre-registration: H3 expected deep
counterfactuals (recovered attention cost κ) to beat as-if counterfactuals
by an order of magnitude, uniformly over markets. **They do not.** We report
this as pre-registered.

## What worked: the deep parameter is identified
The cross-sectional identification strategy succeeds exactly:
**κ̂ = 0.200 (truth 0.200)** recovered by minimum distance on the θ̂₂(s)
profile across five markets (θ̂₂ monotone in stakes: 0.54 → 1.18). The
attention cost — the primitive one level below preferences — is recoverable
from as-if estimates plus observable stakes variation. This half of the
hierarchy thesis stands.

## What failed: plug-in deep prediction, in both variants
| s | as-if RMSE | deep (pooled θ₂) | deep (de-attenuated per market) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.5 | 0.025 | 0.058 | 0.048 |
| 0.75 | 0.012 | 0.070 | 0.066 |
| 1.0 | 0.007 | 0.057 | 0.054 |
| 1.5 | 0.016 | 0.034 | 0.033 |
| 2.0 | 0.022 | 0.008 | 0.011 |

The deep predictions are WORSE than as-if in 4 of 5 markets, in both the
pooled-θ₂ variant and the internally-more-consistent per-market
de-attenuation (θ₂ = θ̂₂(s)/m̂₀).

## Why (the methodological finding)
The as-if parameter vector is a *pseudo-true value*: (θ̂₁, θ̂₂, R̂C) are
chosen JOINTLY by MLE, so their individual biases compensate each other in
prediction (the White/QMLE logic). Replacing one coordinate (θ₂) with a
"deeper" estimate — however well identified — breaks the compensation:
the assembled vector is coherent at no level, and predicts worse than the
honestly-misspecified as-if model. **Estimating one level deeper is
necessary when policy moves the primitive (E1: Spearman 0.88), but it must
be done coherently: full-solution estimation with the attention technology
inside the likelihood (θ₁, θ₂, RC, κ jointly), not two-step plug-in.**
That joint estimator is computationally heavy (the attention fixed point
sits inside the likelihood) and is the paper's designated next experiment.

## Revised thesis statement for the note
The hierarchy of invariance survives — with a sharpened warning label:
1. As-if estimation is robust to fixed primitives (paper #1) and fails
   when policy moves them (E1, Spearman 0.88), invisibly to pre-policy
   audits (E2, Spearman −0.09).
2. The deep primitive is identifiable from cross-sectional stakes variation
   (κ̂ exact).
3. But deep CORRECTION is not free: plug-in hybrids underperform coherent
   misspecification. Depth must be bought wholesale (joint estimation), not
   retail (parameter swapping).
